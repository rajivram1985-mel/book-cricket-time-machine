## Full description (max 4000 chars)

> Remember book cricket? Last bench, boring class, a fat textbook slid across
> the desk. Flip to a random page, read the last digit of the page number, and
> that's the ball: 0 is OUT, 1–6 score that many runs, 7, 8 and 9 sneak a
> single. No bat, no ball, no ground — just paper, luck, and glory.
>
> Book Cricket Time Machine brings that exact ritual back, and then asks the
> question the schoolyard never could: what if Bradman faced Warne? What if
> Tendulkar took on Marshall?
>
> THREE WAYS TO PLAY
>
> 🗓 DAILY CHALLENGE — one seeded chase per day, the same setup for everyone on
> Earth. One attempt, a streak to protect, and a spoiler-free emoji grid to
> share with your group chat.
>
> 📖 CLASSIC — pure page-flip luck, schoolyard rules. Pick a nostalgic book off
> the shelf (or type in your own) and flip. The odds are never adjusted for
> anyone: 0 is out for every player, every time.
>
> 📊 TIME MACHINE — pick your batsman and bowler from 25 cricket legends and
> duel across eras, with every ball weighted by real career numbers. Choose
> your stance before each ball, gamble the once-an-innings power play, and run
> the best-of-3 Gauntlet against ever-tougher rivals. A playful simulation,
> never a prediction — and the "How the odds work" panel shows you exactly
> what's moving the numbers.
>
> THE DETAILS THAT MATTER
>
> • Live commentary from four original voice personas — wickets, boundaries and
>   verdicts only; the quiet balls stay quiet.
> • A career scorebook: wins, streaks, personal bests, your luckiest-ever ball,
>   and the coveted 🦆 early-duck badge.
> • Works offline after the first visit.
> • No accounts, no ads, no tracking. Your scorebook lives only on your device,
>   with a one-tap backup file if you switch phones.
>
> Two minutes a match. Thirty years of nostalgia.

## Store assets

- Listing icon: use `public/icons/icon-512-maskable.png` (full-bleed — Play

  the luck report are the best candidates).

## Console questionnaire notes

- Category: Game → Sports (or Casual — either is defensible).
- Ads: none. In-app purchases: none.
- Data safety: no data collected, no data shared (localStorage only; the
  privacy policy at /privacy.html spells it out).
- Content rating (IARC): answer everything "no" — comes out Everyone / PEGI 3.
- Target audience: select 13+ ONLY. Declaring under-13s as a target audience
  triggers Google's Families policy programme (extra review, restricted
  SDK rules, teacher-approved requirements). The game is safe for kids, but
  the DECLARED target should stay 13+ to avoid that programme entirely —
  kids can still install it; this is a marketing declaration, not a rating.
- Trader status (EU DSA): declaring "not a trader" is fine for a free hobby
  app; it means the app won't be distributed in the EU. Family in India and
  friends in Australia are unaffected.
